Details Of Chandrayaan-1 :


Chandrayaan-1 (transl. Moon-craft, pronunciation (help·info)) was the first Indian lunar probe under the Chandrayaan program. It was launched by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in October 2008 and operated until August 2009. Mission included a lunar orbiter and an impactor. It mapped the Moon in infrared, visible, and X-ray light from lunar orbit and used reflected radiation to prospect for various elements, minerals and ice.

Launch date: 22 October 2008, 00:52 UTC
Launch mass: 1,380 kg (3,040 lb)
Mission duration:
Planned: 2 years (29 January,2009)
Final: 10 months, 6 days (Lost On 29 August,2009)
Mission type: Lunarorbiter

Details Of Chandrayaan-2 :

Chandrayaan-2 (candra-yāna, transl. "MoonCraft"; pronunciation (help·info)) is the second lunar exploration mission developed by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), after Chandrayaan-1. It consists of a lunar orbiter, and also included the Vikram lander, and the Pragyan lunar rover, all of which were developed in India.

Landing date: 6 September 2019, 20:23 UTC
Launch date: 22 July 2019, 09:13:12 UTC
Mission type: Lunarorbiter, lander, rover
Rocket: GSLV Mark III M1

Details Of Chandrayaan-3 :

Chandrayaan-3 will be a mission repeat of Chandrayaan-2 but willonly include a lander and rover similar to that of Chandrayaan-2. It will not have an orbiter. The project is planned to be launched sometime in 2022.

Bus: Chandrayaan
Manufacturer: Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO)
Mission type: Lunarlander, rover, propulsion module
Operator: Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO)
